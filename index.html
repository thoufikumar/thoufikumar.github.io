CASE STUDY 1

SmartMeet

Student–Faculty Appointment Scheduling App

Problem:

Students and faculty face uncertainty when trying to schedule appointments. Without a structured system, students often wait outside offices unsure if faculty are available, while faculty struggle to manage unscheduled drop-ins alongside their planned work.

What I Did:

  • Designed a confirmation-based appointment flow
  • Removed fixed time selection to reduce friction
  • Built clarity around request status and responses

Outcome:

The app reduced in-person waiting by giving students visibility into faculty availability. Faculty gained control over their schedules while students received clear confirmation of their requests.

AcadComp – Academic Companion System

UX Case Study – Completed

Problem:

Students, faculty, and operational staff experienced uncertainty and delays when performing time-sensitive tasks such as attendance validation and meal transactions. Lack of real-time feedback, unclear system responses, and navigation interruptions created operational friction during short but high-pressure interactions.

What I Did:

  • Studied the operational workflows of the KAUST Academy program system to understand real-world constraints
  • Mapped scenario-based journeys for students, faculty, and store staff during attendance and meal processes

  • Designed context-aware interaction flows focused on real-time feedback and minimal navigation during critical tasks

Current Status:

The design exploration and interface prototypes for the system have been completed. The case study documents the full UX process including research, problem definition, and interaction design. Academic Meeting Scheduler

Reducing uncertainty and waiting in academic meetings.

  • UX / UI Designer
  • Mobile App
  • Nov 2024
  • Personal Case Study

Overview

SmartMeet is a scheduling platform that allows students to request and manage faculty meetings without uncertainty, long waits, or manual coordination.

The problem

Students struggled to meet faculty due to unclear availability and unstructured communication.

Why it matters ?

  • Wasted time — Students repeatedly checked for faculty availability without a clear scheduling system
  • Missed meetings — Lack of structured confirmation caused confusion about approved meeting requests
  • Follow-up anxiety — Students were unsure whether faculty had seen or responded to requests

Who it impacts ?

  • Students seeking academic guidance — Needed a reliable way to request and track faculty meetings
  • Faculty managing requests — Faced overlapping meeting requests and unstructured communication channels

Existing Approaches

The Ways used

Chances of a Successful Meet

0% Meets were Successful

Ideation & Process

How I approached it

  • Observed real student behavior
  • Studied existing scheduling patterns
  • Focused on reducing decision friction

Key decisions Made

  • Removed fixed-time requirement
  • Made faculty the final confirmer
  • Prioritized status visibility

Design Execution

Overall App is summarized to 3 main steps:

1
2
3

New meeting request

A Student can..

  • Search faculty – Apply filters
  • Choose reason for the meet
  • See Preview Card
  • Submit Request

Faculty Receives the Request

A Faculty can

  • View urgent, pending, and new requests
  • View today’s meetings
  • Set time & venue for the meet

Student gets Notified

  • Meeting scheduled details
  • Timely reminders

Usability Testing & Validation

Who tested it ?

Tested with a small group of college students and faculty members.

What I observed ?

Early tests showed confusion around request status and response expectations, especially after sending a meeting request.

What changed ?

I introduced clearer status indicators, confirmation messages, and improved feedback after faculty actions.

Final Outcome – The solution focused on clarity, not control.

  • Reduced unnecessary in-person visits
  • Improved clarity around meeting status
  • Positive feedback from test users

CASE STUDY 2

Overview Problem Process Design Validation Outcome

AcadComp

KAUST Program Companion

Reducing uncertainty in attendance validation and meal transactions.

  • UX / UI Designer
  • Mobile App
  • Concept Exploration
  • Personal Case Study

Overview

AcadComp is an operational system designed for KAUST Academy programs that helps students, faculty, and store staff manage attendance validation and meal transactions efficiently during short-term academic programs.

The problem

Students, faculty, and store staff experienced operational friction during attendance scanning and meal purchases due to unclear feedback, slow interactions, and navigation interruptions.

Why it matters ?

  • Attendance uncertainty — Students lacked confirmation that their session attendance was successfully recorded
  • Operational delays — Manual verification slowed attendance tracking and meal service during busy program hours
  • Balance confusion — Students could not clearly track remaining meal allowance after transactions

Who it impacts ?

  • Students attending academy programs — Needed quick QR validation for attendance and meal access
  • Faculty conducting sessions — Required uninterrupted scanning to record attendance for large student groups
  • Store staff processing meals — Needed fast QR transactions to handle high-volume meal purchases

Existing Approaches

  • Manual Attendance – Faculty recorded attendance manually or through spreadsheets, slowing the process and increasing errors.
  • Unstructured Meal Access – Students relied on verbal confirmation or staff checks to access meal allowances.
  • Limited Balance Visibility – Students could not clearly track remaining daily allowance or previous purchases.
  • Operational Delays – Faculty and store staff experienced delays when managing large groups during sessions.
  • Lack of Real-time Feedback – Students were unsure whether attendance scans or transactions were successfully recorded.

Ideation & Process

How I approached it

  • Studied the academy program workflow
  • Mapped real-world operational scenarios
  • Focused on reducing navigation during time-sensitive tasks

Key decisions Made

  • Introduced context-aware home screen
  • Made scanner the primary operational interface
  • Added real-time feedback for validation and transactions

Design Execution

Overall App is summarized to 3 main steps:

1
2
3

Attendance Check-in

  • Scan faculty-provided QR codes
  • Verify presence in real-time
  • View subject-wise attendance

  • Generate unique QR for meals
  • Check daily allowance status
  • View menu and transaction logs

Transaction Tracking

A Student can..

  • View detailed history of all logs
  • Monitor balance and usage
  • Get instant digital receipts

Also the features for Faculty & Store staff

The system also supports faculty and store staff with tools for scanning attendance, tracking sessions, and reviewing transaction summaries efficiently.

Usability Testing & Validation

Who tested it ?

Tested with a small group of students and faculty members in the acad program.

What I observed ?

Early tests showed students sometimes struggled with understanding QR scanning success vs. meal allowance verification.

What changed ?

I introduced explicit confirmation screens and haptic feedback for successful scans to improve confidence.

Final Outcome – A unified operational ecosystem for campus life.

  • Replaced manual papers with automated QR check-ins
  • Integrated meal allowance tracking into a single app
  • Positive feedback from academy staff and students
