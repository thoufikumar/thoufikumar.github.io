CASE STUDY 1

SmartMeet

Student–Faculty Appointment Scheduling App

Problem:

Students and faculty face uncertainty when trying to schedule appointments. Without a structured system, students often wait outside offices unsure if faculty are available, while faculty struggle to manage unscheduled drop-ins alongside their planned work.

What I Did:

  • Designed a confirmation-based appointment flow
  • Removed fixed time selection to reduce friction
  • Built clarity around request status and responses

Outcome:

The app reduced in-person waiting by giving students visibility into faculty availability. Faculty gained control over their schedules while students received clear confirmation of their requests.

CASE STUDY 1

Overview Problem Process Design Validation Outcome

SmartMeet

Student–Faculty Appointment Scheduling App

Reducing uncertainty and waiting in academic meetings.

  • UX / UI Designer
  • Mobile App
  • Nov 2024
  • Personal Case Study

Overview

SmartMeet is a scheduling platform that allows students to request and manage faculty meetings without uncertainty, long waits, or manual coordination.

The problem

Students struggled to meet faculty due to unclear availability and unstructured communication.

Why it matters ?

  • Wasted time – Unclear faculty availability
  • Missed meetings – Lack of confirmation flow
  • Anxiety around follow-ups – No status visibility

Who it impacts ?

  • Students seeking guidance – Unstructured meeting requests
  • Faculty managing requests – Overlapping, unmanaged schedules

Existing Approaches

The Ways used

Chances of a Successful Meet

0% Meets were Successful

Ideation & Process

How I approached it

  • Observed real student behavior
  • Studied existing scheduling patterns
  • Focused on reducing decision friction

Key decisions Made

  • Removed fixed-time requirement
  • Made faculty the final confirmer
  • Prioritized status visibility

Design Execution

Overall App is summarized to 3 main steps:

1
2
3

New meeting request

A Student can..

  • Search faculty – Apply filters
  • Choose reason for the meet
  • See Preview Card
  • Submit Request

Faculty Receives the Request

A Faculty can

  • View urgent, pending, and new requests
  • View today’s meetings
  • Set time & venue for the meet

Student gets Notified

A Student gets

  • Meeting scheduled details
  • Timely reminders
  • Option to cancel the meet

Usability Testing & Validation

Who tested it ?

Tested with a small group of college students and faculty members.

What I observed ?

Early tests showed confusion around request status and response expectations, especially after sending a meeting request.

What changed ?

I introduced clearer status indicators, confirmation messages, and improved feedback after faculty actions.

Final Outcome – The solution focused on clarity, not control.

  • Reduced unnecessary in-person visits
  • Improved clarity around meeting status
  • Positive feedback from test users
